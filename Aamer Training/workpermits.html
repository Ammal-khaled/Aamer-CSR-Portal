What is it?

The Advertiser Permit is an official authorization issued by the UAE Media Council for individuals or entities engaging in promotional or advertising activity on social media platforms, paid or unpaid.

Why was it launched?
  • To regulate social-media advertising activities in the UAE, ensuring transparent and credible content.
  • To protect consumers from misleading ads or unlicensed promotional content.
Scope
  • Covers any social-media account through which promotional content is published on behalf of a third party, whether paid, gifted, or barter.
  • Usually exempt when the creator only promotes their own products/services and no third-party paid promotion is involved.
Requirements & Process
  • Applicant must be a UAE resident or have valid residence visa.
  • Must obtain a business/trade license or freelancer permit first.
  • Then apply for the Advertiser Permit via the UAE Media Council portal.
  • In Abu Dhabi, a local business license via ADDED may be required for influencer/advertiser operations.
Costs & Fees
  • Advertiser Permit: Free for the first three years for citizens/residents in many cases.
  • Business/trade license (foundation): Typical cost AED 5,000–15,000+ per year in UAE.
Validity & Renewal
  • Usually valid for 1 year, renewable.
  • Grace-period extension announced: deadline extended to January 31, 2026.
Non-Compliance Penalties
  • Fines for serious breaches can reach AED 1 million.
  • Local fines in Abu Dhabi up to AED 10,000 for unlicensed activity.
Practical Tips
  • Before partnering with a brand, ensure you hold the permit and display your permit number on your social profile.
  • Keep campaign documents (contracts, invoices, gifts) in case of audit.
  • If operating in Abu Dhabi, check whether you need the local ADDED license as well as the federal media permit.
Summary

The licensing regime for influencers and social-media advertising in the UAE (notably Abu Dhabi) is mandatory. You need both a business/freelance license and the Advertiser Permit. Operating without it risks substantial fines and enforcement action.

CSR Guidance & Scripts

  1. Greet the client warmly and confirm their request type (Work Permit / HR Service).
  2. Ask for Job ID / Application ID, and verify company name and nationality of the worker.
  3. Check which category/service is required (Company File, Job Offer, Work Permit Category, New Work Visa, or Corporate Customer).
  4. Ensure all required documents are ready (degree certificate attested, ID cards, salary/work details, etc.).
  5. Guide the client to submit the request via the Aamer app and explain the expected processing time for their selected service.
  6. Inform the client about service fees and any special conditions (e.g., fees not refundable on rejection, extra fees for card payments).
  7. Confirm the client’s contact information for follow-up and remind them to check the app for updates.
  8. End the call politely, offering any additional assistance or guidance if needed.
Note for CSRs: Always verify nationality and document completeness before directing the client to submit the request. Mention special requirements for PRO First Visit or skilled/unskilled visas if applicable. 
“Hello [Client Name], thank you for contacting Aamer CSR. To proceed with your request for [Work Permit / HR Service], please ensure you have your Job ID / Application ID ready. 

Make sure all required documents, including attested degree certificates and ID cards, are available. 

You can submit your request directly through the Aamer app. Once submitted, our HR team will review it and contact you with next steps. 

Please note the service fees: [mention exact fee based on service], and that fees are not refundable if the request is rejected. 

If you have any questions or need further guidance, feel free to reach out. Thank you for choosing Aamer.”
